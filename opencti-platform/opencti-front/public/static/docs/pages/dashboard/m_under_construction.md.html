Each vulnerability identified is categorized into the following types of vulnerability: * **_Defect-based Vulnerabilities_**, often called *fault* vulnerabilities, are vulnerabilities focused on errors or other flaws in the source code that are specific to one or more versions of one or more products that can be exploited by an adversary. * **_Misconfiguration-based Vulnerabilities_** are those vulnerabilities focused on installation and configuration issues that can be exploited by adversary. For example, the configuration of a weak cryptographic algorithm used by a secure protocol might allow an adversary to perform a Man-in-the-Middle-Attack from which they can then send oversized buffers to a vulnerable product as a means of denying service or as a step to execute malicious code to elevate privileges. * **_Unsupported Product-based Vulnerabilities_** are those vulnerabilities focused on the existence of products (software or hardware) that present a vulnerability risk due to the fact that the vendor no longer provides support, typically meaning that the vendor will no longer provide remedies to successful exploits against the product. As a result, the enterprise is left with few actions: *rip-n-replace* the vulnerable product or institute some form of mitigation that reduces, but not eliminates, the potential risk posed by the unsupported product. |Category | Count | Percentage | |:---|---:|---:| | Defect | 32 | 78.05% | | Configuration | 9 | 21.95% | ### Vulnerabilities By Year The chart below illustrates the distribution of vulnerability instances, identified by the scan of the environment, that occurred for one or more vulnerabilities that were published in a given year by one or more authoritative sources of vulnerability information, such a the [U.S. National Vulnerability Database (NVD)](https://nvd.nist.gov/), operated by the [National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST)](https://www.nist.gov/).
